Fun facts about Texas

  1. Texas is the second largest U.S. state in both area and population.
  2. The state was an independent nation from 1836 to 1845 before joining the U.S.
  3. Dr Pepper was invented in Waco, Texas, in 1885.
  4. Texas has its own power grid, separate from the other U.S. states.
  5. The Texas state capitol in Austin is taller than the U.S. Capitol in Washington, D.C.
  6. The King Ranch in Texas is larger than the entire state of Rhode Island.
  7. The first frozen margarita machine was invented in Dallas in 1971.
  8. More species of bats live in Texas than in any other U.S. state.
  9. Texas is known as the "Lone Star State" as a reminder of its former status as an independent republic.
  10. The state flower is the Bluebonnet, and it's illegal to pick one from public property!

State Flower, Flag, and Motto of Texas

State Flower: Bluebonnet

Photo by David Holifield on Unsplash

The Bluebonnet is the official state flower of Texas, known for its vibrant blue hue and blooming in spring across the Texas landscape.

State Flag: Lone Star Flag

Photo by Lucas Beck on Unsplash

The Texas flag, commonly referred to as the "Lone Star Flag," is a symbol of Texas' fight for independence from Mexico and represents the state's pride and unity.

State Motto: Friendship

The state motto of Texas is "Friendship, this state motto comes from the caddo word "taysha" meaning friend or ally which got misspelled and mispronounced eventually becoming the word texas
